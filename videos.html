TRAILER

Creating a short film was the biggest project in film study. So before editing the movie itself I thought it might be fun to try and create a trailer for the film. Using footage from movie I played around with the features of Premier Pro. The trailer heavily relies on the music/sound to set the mood of the film. This was my first ever edit on Premier Pro, so it was purely experimental.

BLOOPERS

My personal opinion on this video is that its better than the movie itself. Mostly just because it shows the fun the group had filming it. Its a experimental edit, using random music, images and effects. Just a video I thought Id share to show you what happened behind the scenes of the filming process.

SHORT FILM

Using Premier Pro my team and I created a Charlie Chaplin insipired dark comedy, Sunday Girl, it is a silent comedy that follows the story of a young girl being chased by a killer. We chose this for various reasons, but mostly because we did not have sound equipment and had to improvise. It also made it easier to deal with lighting during shoots, because everything was edited to be in black and white. This was probably the most fun group project ever and I highly recommend you to watch the Bloopers.

TITLE SEQUENCE

I always loved the Grim Brothers versions of fairytales, therefore I absolutely loved the darker, thriller adaption of Red Riding Hood. I wanted to combine the innocence of a fairytale with a earie look. I decided to create little vectors and style it with textures, to make it look like paper puppets. Combining the innocent look of pupppets with effects and the right music, the title sequence of Red Riding Hood was born.

SELF PROMO
